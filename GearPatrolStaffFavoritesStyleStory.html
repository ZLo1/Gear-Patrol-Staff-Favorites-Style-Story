THE GEAR WE CAN’T LIVE WITHOUT

Staff Picks: Zen Love, Associate Watch Editor Applicant

Much of my appreciation for products of all kinds owes a lot to learning about watches. To me, watches represent something meant to last, a deceptively simple item that is in fact sophisticated in its details. In some small way, I see a well-made watch as a psychological antidote to much of the wasteful emptiness of modern life. I suppose any type of product can have these qualities in some way or another, and each item below is something that I admire or find satisfying in a deeper sense than its mere usefulness or convenience.

TAG Heuer Carrera TwinTime GMT

This is the first mechanical watch I ever owned which led me to appreciating, collecting, and eventually writing about watches. The TAG Heuer Carrera TwinTime is priceless to me for those reasons. But it is also ridiculously versatile, wearable, and with its GMT hand displaying a second time zone, it’s eminently useful especially for a global citizen like myself. I wore this thing every day and beat the hell out of it before I really understood watches, but it has endured in every sense. After all these years, I can still find virtually nothing to criticize about it.

Maui Jim Kahuna Sunglasses

I am a one-pair-of-sunglasses guy since owning these admittedly fancy Maui Jim Kahunas. I find them particularly indispensable for highway driving, and handy for walking around on bright mornings. I also find them to look particularly dashing. Maui Jim uses the best glass in the business, and achieves an experience I would describe as something like HDR mixed with increased vibrance, warmth, and maybe even some kind of sharpening, in photography terms. A worthy luxury.

Herman Miller Aeron Chair

Yoga balls and standing desks are all fine and good. But if you work on a computer and choose to sit in a chair, the Herman Miller Aeron is probably the best way to do it and a worthwhile investment. With different sizes available for different body types, it then further adjusts to exactly the fit you need. Don’t take my word for it, the Herman Miller Aeron is well-known and Gear Patrol has called it “The Chair Against Which All Chairs Are Measured.”

The Economist Magazine

No one packs insight and clarity into a pithy sentence like The Economist magazine does. The  British “newspaper,” as they say, employs some of the best minds analyzing news and politics, and such people have no need for fluff. The Economist helps keep me abreast of current events and geopolitical happenings weekly with some distance from the day-to-day drama and shouting. It is a delight and inspiration to read, as well as a lesson in editorial excellence.

Canon 24mm f2.8 STM “Pancake” Lens 

This has become the lens I basically leave on my Canon 70D most of the time for travel and casual photography. It is cheap, compact, and extremely versatile. It works well for ambience, wristshots of watches, and even macro. Picture quality might not be quite like that of a high-end lens but that just means your pictures all the more reflect your skill.

G-Shock G56000-E

There is nothing else out there quite like a black Casio G-Shock 5600 that is a direct descendent of the original indestructible Japanese wonder watch born in the 1980s. The comfort, the toughness, the casualness, the youthfulness, the nostalgia, the ease, the design, the pragmatic style, the functionality... unbeatable! A G-Shock is totally unpretentious and a watch you never need to worry about: it won’t break but is inexpensive even if it does. Versions with Casio’s Tough Solar technology means you never have to change the battery, and any G-Shock I get must have a positive display because legibility is important. Someday, I will write a poem to G-Shocks.

The Grail

Zapata Flyboard Air

Apparently, this real-life hoverboard by French hydro- and jet-powered technologies company Zapata actually exists, i.e, it’s not just a concept but has been produced. And who wouldn’t want one? It’s expensive, dangerous, and currently only flies for seven minutes at a time. But by the time I manage to actually get one, its future generations will be refined and improved. This company also makes things like jetpacks, but the Flyboard Air is the one for me.
